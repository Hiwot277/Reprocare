EMERGENCY PILL

The emergency pill, also known as the morning-after pill,
is a type of birth control used to prevent pregnancy after unprotected sex.
It is most effective when taken within 72 hours of intercourse,
but can be taken up to 120 hours after.
...

Advantages of the emergency pill include:

It can prevent an unintended pregnancy.
It is easily accessible and available without a prescription.
It can be taken as soon as possible after intercourse.

Disadvantages of the emergency pill include:

It can cause side effects like nausea, vomiting, and irregular bleeding.
It does not protect against sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
It is less effective than other forms of birth control.
