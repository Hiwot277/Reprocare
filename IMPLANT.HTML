CONTRACEPTION

IMPLANT

Implants are a type of contraceptive method that involves inserting a small, flexible rod under the skin of the upper arm.
The implant releases hormones that prevent pregnancy by stopping ovulation, thickening the cervical mucus to prevent sperm from reaching an egg, and thinning the lining of the uterus to make it less receptive to implantation.
...

Advantages of using implants as a contraceptive method include:

their high effectiveness in preventing pregnancy, their long-lasting nature (typically lasting for up to three years), and their convenience, as they require no daily maintenance or upkeep.
implants are discreet and can be easily hidden under the skin, allowing for a more private and personalized contraceptive experience.

disadvantages of using implants include :

the need for a healthcare provider to insert and remove the implant,
the potential for irregular bleeding or spotting, and the potential for side effects such as headaches, mood changes, or weight gain.
all hormonal contraceptive methods, implants may not be suitable for individuals with certain medical conditions or risk factors.
As with any contraceptive method, it's important to discuss your individual needs and preferences with a healthcare provider to determine what method works best for you.
