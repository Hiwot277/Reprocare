CONTRACEPTION

Contraception refers to methods or devices used to prevent pregnancy by blocking or reducing the chances of fertilization or implantation of a fertilized egg.

...

Advantage of contraceptive method

The main advantage of using a contraceptive method is:
  • it allows individuals to control their reproductive health and prevent unintended pregnancy.
  • This can help to reduce the risk of sexually transmitted infections (STIs).
  • prevent complications during pregnancy and childbirth, and promote overall health and wellbeing.
  • Additionally, many contraceptive methods can be used to regulate menstrual cycles, reduce menstrual cramps and pain, and improve acne or other hormonal-related conditions.
  • Using a contraceptive method can also provide peace of mind and reduce anxiety related to the possibility of unintended pregnancy.

Disadvantage of contraceptive method

One disadvantage of using a contraceptive method is that it may have side effects.
These can vary depending on the type of method used and may include ;
  • changes in menstrual bleeding patterns, headaches, nausea, weight gain, and mood changes.
  • Some individuals may also experience allergic reactions or other complications from certain methods.
  • some methods may require regular maintenance or monitoring, which can be inconvenient or costly.
  • some individuals may have personal or cultural beliefs that prevent them from using certain contraceptive methods.
